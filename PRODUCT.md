# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Orange County homeowners whose appliance has broken down. They are in mild-to-moderate urgency mode — fridge stopped cooling, washer won't spin, oven died before dinner. They search on their phone, skim for trust signals (credentials, reviews, response time), and want to book a same-day or next-day appointment fast. They are not technical; they want confidence that the repair will be done right, at a fair price, by someone local and accountable.

## Product Purpose

A conversion-focused local service website. Its job is to turn a stressed homeowner into a booked appointment or phone call. Success means the visitor finds the right service, believes the business is trustworthy and competent, and contacts them — ideally within 60 seconds of arriving on the page. Secondary goal: rank well in local Google search so the site is found in the first place.

## Brand Personality

Warm, competent, straight-talking. Family-owned feel without being folksy. The business is run by people who know appliances and genuinely care about their neighbors. Not a national chain. Not a slick tech startup. A real local expert who shows up on time, explains the issue clearly, and charges fairly.

Three words: **Trustworthy. Local. Skilled.**

Emotional goals: confidence (this person knows what they're doing), relief (the problem will be solved quickly), and goodwill (this is a business worth recommending to friends).

## Anti-references

No explicit anti-references provided. Avoid the aesthetics of: large national home-service aggregators (generic, franchise-cold), corporate HVAC chains (sterile, impersonal), or overdesigned local service sites that feel more like a tech startup than a tradesperson. The site should feel like a real business, not a template.

## Design Principles

1. **Trust before pitch.** Credentials, reviews, and proof of work come before promotional copy. A homeowner's first question is "can I trust this person in my home?" — answer that question on the first scroll.
2. **Clarity over cleverness.** The visitor is stressed. Every heading, label, and CTA must be instantly understood. No abstract taglines, no unexplained jargon.
3. **Local warmth, not corporate polish.** The family-owned, community-rooted identity is a genuine differentiator. Design choices should reinforce personality, not erase it in pursuit of appearing "professional."
4. **Conversion at every scroll.** The phone number and booking path are never more than one action away. On mobile, the sticky bar ensures this is always true.
5. **Mobile-first, always.** The majority of visitors arrive on a phone with a broken appliance. Every layout decision should pass the "stressed person on a small screen" test first.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance recommended as the baseline. Key needs: sufficient color contrast for text over orange and dark backgrounds, keyboard-navigable nav and FAQ accordion, minimum 44×44px tap targets on all interactive elements, proper heading hierarchy, and labeled form inputs. No specific reduced-motion or color-blindness accommodations flagged, but standard good practice applies.
