Dryer Repair Cost in Orange County, CA — 2025 Pricing Guide

Home › Blog › Dryer Repair Cost — Orange County 2025

Dryer repair in Orange County costs less than most homeowners expect — and far less than buying new. This guide gives you real 2025 price ranges for every common dryer problem, so you know exactly what to budget before you call.

After completing thousands of dryer repairs across Orange County — from Anaheim and Irvine to Huntington Beach and Santa Ana — we've seen the same failures repeat over and over. The pricing is consistent, the diagnoses are predictable, and in the vast majority of cases, repair is the right move. Here's what you'll actually pay.

Dryer Repair Cost by Problem Type

Prices below reflect total cost — parts and labor — for a standard service visit in Orange County. A diagnostic fee of $75–$100 is charged upfront and applied toward the repair if you proceed.

Electric Dryers

Thermal Fuse Replacement

Most common cause of a dryer that runs but produces no heat. Usually caused by a clogged vent restricting airflow.

$80–$140
~45 min

Heating Element Replacement

The coiled wire that generates heat. Fails gradually — dryer takes longer to dry before stopping entirely.

$120–$220
~60 min

Cycling Thermostat Replacement

Regulates operating temperature. A faulty thermostat causes overheating or no heat at all.

$100–$180
~45 min

Drive Belt Replacement

If the drum doesn't spin but the motor runs, the belt has snapped. Common after 7–10 years of use.

$90–$160
~60 min

Drum Bearing / Roller Replacement

Loud squealing or thumping during operation. Bearings wear out over time and need replacement before they damage the drum.

$130–$240
~90 min

Control Board Replacement

Dryer won't start, stops randomly, or displays error codes. Most common on digital control models after power surges.

$200–$380
~60–90 min

Motor Replacement

Dryer hums but won't start, or stops after a few minutes. Motor replacement is one of the pricier repairs.

$220–$400
~2–3 hrs
Gas Dryers

Gas Valve Coil Replacement

Gas dryer runs but produces no heat. The igniter glows but the gas valve coils fail to open the valve.

$120–$200
~60 min

Igniter Replacement

The igniter glows red to light the gas burner. When it fails, the burner never lights and the dryer blows cold air.

$100–$180
~45–60 min

Flame Sensor Replacement

Shuts off the gas if the flame goes out. A faulty sensor prevents the burner from staying lit.

$90–$160
~45 min
💡 Most common repair across OC: The thermal fuse, at $80–$140, accounts for roughly 35% of all dryer repairs we perform in Orange County. It's almost always triggered by a lint-clogged dryer vent — which means cleaning your vent annually can prevent the most frequent (and completely avoidable) dryer repair call.

What Affects the Final Price

The prices above are ranges for a reason. Several factors shift the final cost:

1

Brand and Model

Parts for Samsung, LG, and Bosch tend to cost more than equivalent parts for Whirlpool or Maytag. High-end brands like Miele or Electrolux can push repair costs 20–40% higher due to proprietary parts and longer labor times.

2

Electric vs. Gas

Gas dryer repairs typically run $20–$50 higher than equivalent electric repairs due to the additional complexity of the gas system and the need for a gas-certified technician.

3

Parts Availability

If your dryer model is discontinued or the required part isn't in stock locally, the technician may need to order it — adding 1–3 days and occasionally a parts premium. Sticking to major brands (Whirlpool, GE, Samsung, LG) means faster, cheaper part sourcing.

4

Secondary Failures

A clogged vent that burned out the thermal fuse may have also damaged the heating element. When a technician finds secondary damage during a repair, expect the quote to increase. Always ask if secondary components should be inspected when you receive your initial estimate.

When Is Dryer Repair Worth It in Orange County?

The standard benchmark is the 50% rule: if the repair cost exceeds 50% of the replacement cost, buying new is usually smarter. A mid-range dryer in 2025 costs $600–$900 installed across Orange County, so repairs under $350–$450 are almost always worth it.

Age is the other key variable. Most dryers are designed for 10–13 years. If your machine is 11 years old and needs a $300 motor replacement, that's a reasonable repair — but if it also needs a belt, bearings, and thermostat in the same visit, the combined cost may tip the scales toward replacement.

The one exception: premium brands. A Speed Queen or Miele dryer is engineered for 20–25 years of use. Spending $350 to keep one of those machines running another decade is an easy call.

⚠️ Before you book: Clean your dryer vent before the technician arrives. A clogged vent is the root cause of the most common dryer failures — and if the vent isn't cleared, a newly replaced thermal fuse will blow again within weeks. Vent cleaning runs $80–$130 as a separate service, or ask us to include it with your repair visit.

Frequently Asked Questions — Dryer Repair Cost in Orange County

How much does dryer repair cost in Orange County?

Dryer repair in Orange County typically runs $100–$400 depending on the problem. A diagnostic fee of $75–$100 is charged upfront and applied toward the repair. Simple fixes like a thermal fuse run $80–$140; major repairs like motor or control board replacement run $200–$400.

Is it worth repairing a dryer in Orange County?

Yes, in most cases. If the repair is under 50% of a new dryer's cost and the machine is under 10 years old, repair is almost always the better financial choice. We'll give you an honest recommendation — if it doesn't make sense to repair, we'll tell you.

Why is my dryer not heating?

The most common cause is a blown thermal fuse, which trips when the dryer overheats — usually due to a clogged vent. Other causes include a failed heating element (electric), faulty gas valve coils (gas dryers), or a defective cycling thermostat. A technician can pinpoint the exact component in 20–30 minutes.

How long does dryer repair take in Orange County?

Most repairs are completed in a single visit of 1–2 hours. We carry the most common parts for Samsung, LG, Whirlpool, Maytag, and GE in our service vans, which covers the majority of Orange County repair calls without needing to order parts.
