Hard Water & Dishwasher Performance in Orange County

Orange County tap water is classified as moderately to very hard — ranging from 200 to 400+ ppm in many service areas. Over time, calcium and magnesium deposits build up inside your dishwasher, clogging spray arm holes, coating the heating element, and leaving a white film on dishes that no detergent can remove.

💧 Signs your dishwasher has a hard water problem

White chalky residue on glasses and the interior walls, reduced water pressure from the spray arms, dishes that feel gritty even after a hot cycle, or a dishwasher that uses more detergent than before without better results.

What helps: Use a rinse aid in every cycle, run a dishwasher cleaner monthly, and consider a whole-home water softener if scale is a recurring issue. During a service visit, we descale the interior and clear blocked spray arm jets as part of a standard clean-and-inspect.

Repair or Replace Your Dishwasher?

Dishwashers typically last 9–12 years. If your machine is under 8 years old, repair almost always makes financial sense — quality replacement units run $600–$1,200 installed, plus the cost and hassle of removal and delivery. Common repairs (drain pump, door latch, spray arm, inlet valve) are a fraction of that.

Our rule of thumb: If the repair is under 50% of what a comparable new dishwasher costs and the machine is under 10 years old, fix it. Over 12 years old with a failed control board or motor, we'll tell you honestly — and help you decide rather than push for the repair.

The most common causes are a clogged drain filter, a blocked drain hose, or a failed drain pump. If your dishwasher connects to a garbage disposal, a clogged disposal or a newly installed one with the knockout plug still in place will also prevent drainage. Check and clean the filter first — it takes two minutes and fixes most drain calls.
Leaks typically come from a worn or cracked door gasket, a loose water inlet valve, a faulty float switch, or a cracked door latch that prevents a proper seal. Leaks from the bottom of the door are almost always the gasket. Leaks from under the unit usually point to the inlet valve or pump seal.
Dirty dishes after a full wash usually come from clogged spray arms (mineral deposits block the holes), a failing wash pump, a detergent dispenser that doesn't open mid-cycle, or water temperature that's too low. In Orange County, hard water accelerates spray arm clogging — cleaning them monthly with white vinegar helps.
Grinding during the wash cycle usually means broken glass or debris is caught in the pump impeller. A loud hum with no water movement points to a seized pump motor. Rattling is often an unsecured utensil hitting the spray arm. A technician can isolate the exact cause in one visit.
