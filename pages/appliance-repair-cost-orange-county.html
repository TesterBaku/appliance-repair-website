What Affects the Repair Cost?

Five variables account for most price variation between similar repairs.

🔧
The Specific Failure
A faulty thermostat costs far less than a compressor. The symptom matters, but the root cause matters more. Diagnosis pins this down before any commitment.
🏷️
Brand & Parts Availability
Whirlpool, GE, and Samsung parts are widely stocked. Sub-Zero, Viking, and Thermador parts are often proprietary and may need to be ordered — adding time and cost.
📅
Age of the Appliance
Older units may have multiple worn components. A single trip can address the primary failure, but secondary issues sometimes surface after the repair — we flag these in the estimate.
⚙️
Repair Complexity
Sealed-system refrigerator work and smart-appliance control boards take more labor than a belt or latch replacement. Labor time scales with how deep the technician has to go into the unit.
🚚
Parts on the Truck
Common parts for the most popular brands are stocked on our vehicles — this enables same-visit repairs without added cost. Specialty parts may require a follow-up visit after ordering.
📍
Same-Day Service
We don't charge a premium for same-day appointments. The price is the same whether you call today or schedule a week out — availability is the only variable.

Repair or Replace? A Practical Guide

The 50% rule: if the repair cost exceeds 50% of a comparable replacement installed, replacement is usually the smarter move.

Lean toward repair when…

Repair Makes Sense

  • Appliance is under 5 years old
  • Repair cost is under 50% of replacement
  • The failure is mechanical, not systemic
  • The brand is premium (Sub-Zero, Wolf) — replacement cost is very high
  • You've been happy with its performance
Lean toward replacement when…

Replacement Makes Sense

  • Appliance is over 10–12 years old
  • Repair cost exceeds 50% of a new unit installed
  • This is the second major repair in two years
  • The compressor, motor, or sealed system has failed
  • Parts are discontinued or no longer available

Not sure? We'll give you an honest read at the diagnostic visit — including whether repair is economical given the appliance's age and condition. No pressure either way.
