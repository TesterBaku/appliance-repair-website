Miele Models We Service

From the G5000 series dishwasher to the latest H7000 wall oven, we service every generation of Miele appliances in Orange County.

G7000 and G5000 Dishwashers
Crystal, Diamond, and Visions series dishwashers. The G7000 added the AutoDos PowerDisk system and WiFi connectivity. F11 drain faults and door latch failures are the most common service calls on these models.
Futura Classic and Futura Crystal
Pre-G5000 dishwashers still common in OC homes from 2010-2016. Well-built units that are still worth repairing. Drain pump and heating element replacements are the most frequent calls.
W1 Classic and WX1 Washing Machines
Front-loading washers with honeycomb drum design. Drum bearing wear is the most significant long-term failure, presenting as a banging noise during spin. Door interlock and drain pump faults are more common shorter-term issues.
T1 Classic and TX1 Heat-Pump Dryers
Heat-pump dryers that recirculate air rather than venting. Condenser filter blockage and heat exchanger fouling are the most common service issues. Drum motor and control board faults also occur.
H7000 and H6000 Wall Ovens
M-Touch and visual-control wall ovens. The M-Touch control panel is the most common failure point: blank display, unresponsive touch zones, or flickering. We also service heating elements, fans, and door hinges.
CM6, CM7, and CVA Coffee Systems
Built-in and countertop automatic coffee systems. Brew unit failures, grinder jams, and descaling sensor faults are the most frequent service calls. CVA built-in models require cabinet access handled carefully to avoid cabinetry damage.

Common Miele Problems We Fix

Miele appliances are exceptionally reliable, but they develop recognizable failure patterns. Here's what we diagnose most often across Orange County.

● F11 Error (Drain Fault) The most common Miele dishwasher service call. The machine could not drain within the expected time. A blocked drain pump, clogged filter basket, or kinked drain hose can all trigger F11. We clear blockages first before recommending a pump replacement.
● F19 Error (Heating / NTC Sensor) The heating element failed to raise water temperature to the target range, or the NTC temperature sensor is giving a false reading. We test both before replacing either component to avoid unnecessary parts costs.
● F40 / F41 Washer Error (Drain or Fill) F40 indicates a fill fault, F41 a drain fault on W1 series washers. The drain pump, water inlet valve, and pressure switch are the typical suspects. Most are diagnosed and resolved in one service visit.
● Door Interlock Micro-Switch Failure The dishwasher or washer door appears closed but the machine won't start, or it interrupts mid-cycle. The door interlock micro-switch fails after thousands of open-close cycles. Replacement is straightforward and restores normal operation.
● Water Inlet Valve Scale-Up OC water hardness accelerates mineral scale buildup on the water inlet valve screen and orifice. The machine fills slowly or triggers F47 fill errors. Descaling or valve replacement resolves it.
● Drum Bearing Wear on W1 Washers A rhythmic banging or grinding during the spin cycle points to worn drum bearings. This is the most labor-intensive Miele washer repair. The job requires disassembling the cabinet and drum. Worth doing given Miele's parts longevity.
● M-Touch Control Panel Failure The H7000 oven display goes blank, flickers, or stops responding to touch. The M-Touch panel integrates the display and control logic in one assembly. Replacement requires sourcing the correct panel for the specific model year.
● AutoDos Detergent Dispenser Solenoid On G7000 models with the PowerDisk AutoDos system, the dispenser solenoid can fail to open at the correct cycle phase. The dishwasher runs but dishes come out with detergent residue or poorly cleaned. Solenoid replacement restores proper dosing.
