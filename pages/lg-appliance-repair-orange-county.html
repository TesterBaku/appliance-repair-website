Compressor Failures, Error Codes, and LG's Known Weak Points

LG appliances fail in specific, recognizable ways. These are the repairs our technicians handle most often on Orange County homes.

Refrigerator Not Cooling (Linear Compressor) The freezer thaws and the fresh-food side drifts warm, often with no error code. On 2014 and later LG fridges this usually points to the linear compressor, which is frequently still under LG's extended 10-year parts warranty. We confirm the fault before you replace the fridge.
Washer LE Motor Error The direct-drive motor is overloaded or locked and the cycle stops. Causes range from a tangled load to a worn rotor and stator, a failed Hall sensor, or a seized bearing. We test each part before quoting so you do not replace a whole motor for a sensor.
Washer OE Drain Error The washer cannot pump water out. Almost always a clogged drain-pump filter full of lint and coins, and clearing it fixes many cases. If it is clean, the drain pump has failed or the hose is kinked. We find the exact blockage.
Washer UE / Shaking and Walking A UE on every load, with violent shaking or a washer that walks, usually means worn shock absorbers, tired suspension, or a failing bearing. We recently swapped the shocks on an LG front-loader in Fountain Valley for exactly this fault.
Dryer Won't Heat Tumbles but blows cold. On electric models it is the heating element or a thermistor; on gas it is the igniter or valve coil. A blocked vent overheats and trips the cutoff, so we check airflow as part of every no-heat repair.
Dishwasher OE or IE Fault Standing water (OE) or a dishwasher that won't fill (IE) traces to a clogged filter, a jammed drain pump, or a failed inlet valve. We clear the blockage and confirm the pump and valve before returning the unit to service.
Ice Maker or Water Dispenser Fault An LG ice maker that stops filling is often the water inlet valve, the ice-maker assembly, or a frozen fill tube, not the whole door. We test the valve and assembly so you are not paying to replace a part that still works.
Oven Won't Reach Temperature A bake element that stays cold, a weak gas igniter, or uneven baking usually means a failed element or a drifting temperature sensor. We meter the element and sensor to pin down which one before quoting parts.

The LG Linear Compressor: Why LG Refrigerators Stop Cooling

If your LG refrigerator slowly gave up, the freezer thawed, and the food spoiled with no dramatic noise or error on the display, you are not imagining it, and you are far from alone. The heart of nearly every modern LG refrigerator is the linear inverter compressor, a design LG markets heavily for being quiet and efficient. It genuinely is both. But on a large number of 2014 and later models, that compressor has also proven prone to failing early, and when it fails the fridge simply stops getting cold.

Here is the part most Orange County homeowners do not know, and the reason it is worth a phone call before you haul the fridge to the curb. LG extended the warranty on the linear compressor to 10 years for parts on many affected models. So even a refrigerator that feels too old to bother with often still has a covered compressor. That completely changes the repair-or-replace decision, because the most expensive single part may cost you nothing.

What we do is diagnose honestly. A warm LG fridge is not always the compressor: it can be the sealed system elsewhere, the evaporator fan, a defrost fault, or a control board, and each of those is a very different repair and price. We confirm exactly which one it is, tell you plainly whether the compressor is likely under LG's warranty, and handle the labor to get you cold again. When the numbers say replace instead, we tell you that too. Either way you decide with real information, not a guess.

LE is a motor error on LG direct-drive washers: the motor is overloaded or locked and the control board has stopped the cycle. The common causes are an overloaded or badly tangled load, a worn rotor and stator, a failed Hall sensor that tells the board where the motor is, or a seized bearing. Sometimes it clears with a lighter load, but a repeating LE points to a real motor-side fault. We test the rotor, stator, sensor, and bearing to find which one failed before quoting, so you are not paying for a whole motor when a sensor is the problem.
OE is a drain error: the machine could not pump the water out in time. On an LG washer the number one cause is a clogged drain-pump filter packed with lint, coins, and hair, and clearing that filter fixes many cases with no parts at all. If the filter is clean and water still stands, the drain pump has failed, the drain hose is kinked, or the pressure sensor is misreading. The same code on an LG dishwasher usually means a blocked filter, a jammed drain pump, or a clogged drain line. We find the exact blockage and repair it, usually the same day.
UE means the load is unbalanced and the machine cannot safely reach spin speed. An occasional UE on a single heavy item, like a comforter, is normal. But a UE that shows on every load, along with violent shaking or a washer that walks across the laundry room, usually means the shock absorbers and suspension have worn out or a spider arm and bearing are failing.
