Drain Faults, Flood Lockouts, and Bosch's Known Weak Points

Bosch appliances fail in specific, recognizable ways. These are the repairs our technicians handle most often on Orange County kitchens.

Dishwasher Won't Drain (E24 / E25) Standing water at the bottom of the tub. Nearly always a clogged filter or a blocked drain hose, and clearing the filter fixes many cases. If it is clean, the drain pump impeller is jammed or the pump has failed. We find the exact blockage point.
E15 Flood-Float Lockout Water in the base pan tripped the anti-flood float, so the machine locks out and runs the drain pump to protect your floor. Draining the pan clears it, but a hose, sump, or door-seal leak put the water there. We fix the source so it does not return.
Dishes Come Out Wet Often normal, not a fault. Bosch dries by condensation with no heating element, so plastics stay damp and rinse aid is required. If you use rinse aid and dishes are still soaked, the heating circuit or rinse-aid dispenser has failed, and we test both.
Dishwasher Won't Start or No Power A dark panel points to a tripped outlet, a failed door latch, or a control-board fault. One that lights but won't run usually is not latching. Bosch's top-edge hidden controls make a cracked ribbon cable or worn latch a frequent cause.
Induction Zone Won't Detect Pans A cooktop zone that flashes or won't heat despite correct cookware. The fault is usually the power control board or a touch-control sensor rather than the coil itself. We measure under operating load to avoid replacing a good coil.
Compact Washer Won't Spin or Drain On the 24-inch front-loaders, a blocked drain pump, a failed door lock, or an unbalanced load stops the spin. These stack-and-closet units are tight to service, and we carry the common Bosch pumps and locks.
Heat-Pump Dryer Not Drying Ventless Bosch dryers reclaim heat through a condenser that clogs with lint over time. A dryer that runs long, leaves clothes damp, or leaks water usually needs the condenser cleaned or the heating circuit repaired.
Oven Not Heating or Off-Temperature A Bosch wall oven that won't reach temperature or bakes unevenly usually has a failed bake element, a drifting temperature sensor, or a control fault. We meter the element and sensor to pin down which one before quoting.

Why Your Bosch Dishwasher Leaves Plastics Wet (and When It's Actually Broken)

This is the single most common Bosch "problem" that isn't one. Almost every other dishwasher has an exposed heating coil in the floor that bakes the dishes dry at the end of the cycle. Bosch took that coil out. Instead, the machine relies on the heat left over from a hot final rinse, then lets that warmth condense against the cool stainless steel tub, run down the walls, and drain away. It is quieter, gentler on plastics, and more energy efficient, but it dries differently than you may expect.

Two things make condensation drying work, and skipping either one leaves you with a puddle in the cups. First, you have to use a rinse aid such as Jet-Dry, because it breaks the surface tension so water sheets off instead of beading up. Second, glass and ceramic hold heat and dry beautifully, while lightweight plastics cool too fast to shed their moisture, which is why the Tupperware lid on the top rack is still wet when the plates are bone dry. That is the system working as designed, not a fault.

Bosch's higher tiers narrow the gap. The 500 series pops the door open an inch or two at the end to vent the humid air, and the 800 and Benchmark series add CrystalDry, a cartridge of zeolite minerals that pulls moisture from the air and converts it to heat, drying even plastics. So when a customer in Irvine or Newport Beach tells us the dishwasher "stopped drying," we first confirm the model, the rinse-aid level, and the cycle. If you are using rinse aid, running an Auto or heavy cycle, and dishes still come out soaked, then something real has failed, usually the heating circuit that warms the rinse water or the rinse-aid dispenser, and that we can test and repair.
