Ranges & Ovens

Lid Locks, No-Heat Dryers, and Maytag's Known Weak Points

Maytag appliances fail in specific, recognizable ways. These are the repairs our technicians handle most often on Orange County homes.

Washer F5 E2 Lid-Lock Error The Bravos or Centennial fills but won't spin, and the lid clicks without locking. Almost always a failed lid-lock assembly, sometimes the striker or a board misread. It is the most common Maytag washer fault, and we carry the part.
Washer Won't Drain or Spin Water stands in the tub. On top-loaders it is a failed lid lock, a clogged drain pump, or a worn drive; on the Maxima front-loader it is usually the door lock. We test each part before quoting so you pay only for the fault.
Dryer Won't Heat Tumbles but blows cold. On electric models it is a blown thermal fuse, thermostat, or heating element; on gas it is the igniter or valve coil. A blocked vent overheats and trips the fuse, so we check airflow on every no-heat repair.
Bravos Loud or No High-Speed Spin Grinding, rumbling, or a slow spin usually means a worn tub bearing or a failing drive system on the direct-drive motor. Left alone a bad bearing can damage the tub, so we inspect it early and advise honestly.
Refrigerator Not Cooling A warm fresh-food section points to a failed evaporator fan, a stuck defrost system icing the coils, or a worn compressor relay. We diagnose the sealed system precisely rather than guessing at the most expensive part first.
Ice Maker Not Working A Maytag ice maker that stops filling is often the water inlet valve, the ice-maker module, or a frozen fill tube, not the whole assembly. We test the valve and module so you are not paying to replace a part that still works.
Dishwasher Won't Drain or Clean Standing water or gritty dishes usually trace to a clogged filter, a jammed drain pump, or a failed wash motor. We clear the blockage and confirm the pump and inlet valve before returning the unit to service.
Oven Won't Reach Temperature A bake element that stays cold, a weak gas igniter, or uneven baking usually means a failed element or a drifting temperature sensor. We meter the element and sensor to pin down which one before quoting parts.

Maytag and the Whirlpool Connection: Why It Matters for Your Repair

Maytag still carries its old reputation for dependability, the lonely Maytag repairman who never had anything to do. What most Orange County homeowners do not realize is that since 2006, Maytag has been owned and built by Whirlpool Corporation, the largest appliance maker in the country. Today's Maytag rolls off shared platforms alongside Whirlpool, KitchenAid, and Amana, using many of the same control boards, drain pumps, lid locks, heating elements, and drive systems.

That connection matters the moment something breaks. A Maytag Bravos top-loader and a Whirlpool Cabrio are close cousins under the lid, and a Maytag Maxima front-loader shares its platform with the Whirlpool Duet. So a technician who genuinely knows Whirlpool already knows Maytag, and the lid lock or pump that fixes your Maytag washer is often the exact part that fixes the Whirlpool next door. We carry those common parts on the van precisely because they cross both brands, which usually means a faster, one-visit repair.

The practical upshot is simple. Maytag parts are widely available and reasonably priced because they are shared across the Whirlpool family, so most Maytag repairs are affordable and worth doing. If you are not even sure whether your machine is a Maytag or a Whirlpool, read us the model number when you call. It tells us the platform, the likely failure, and the parts to bring, so the first visit is the visit that fixes it.
