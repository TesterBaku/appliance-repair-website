Common Queries Answered

Got a question? We're here to assist. Find answers to the most frequently asked questions about our appliance repair services.

Universal Appliances Repair handles multiple household appliances. Our team addresses issues with refrigerators, ovens, washers, dryers, dishwashers, and additional units. Technicians possess the capability to diagnose and fix problems across all these categories. If you're unsure whether we service your appliance, give us a call at (949) 629-5365.
Repair duration depends on the problem's complexity and the availability of parts. Our technicians strive to complete repairs efficiently without compromising quality. While some repairs may only take a few hours, others may need more time for a thorough and long-lasting fix. We'll give you a realistic time estimate before we begin.
Yes, we offer a warranty on our repairs. We stand behind the quality of our work and want our customers to have peace of mind. Our warranty covers both parts and labor for a specified period. If complications emerge post-repair, we handle corrections at no supplementary expense to you.
You can schedule an appointment through telephone or our website contact form. Simply call us at (949) 629-5365 or fill out the form on our Contact page. Our responsive team will assist you in arranging a convenient appointment time.
Yes! We offer same-day service when a technician is available in your area. For the fastest response, we recommend calling us directly at (949) 629-5365. We serve Orange County and surrounding areas and will do our best to get to you quickly.
Absolutely. Universal Appliances Repair is a fully licensed, bonded, and insured repair service. As a family-owned and operated business, we take pride in being a trusted and professional service provider in the Orange County community.
We primarily serve Orange County, California, including cities like Stanton, Irvine, Anaheim, Santa Ana, Garden Grove, Huntington Beach, and surrounding areas. Not sure if we cover your location? Give us a call and we'll let you know.
Repair cost depends on the appliance type, the nature of the problem, and the parts required. Simple fixes — like a door seal or a clogged drain — cost considerably less than a compressor or motor replacement. We provide a clear estimate before any work begins, so there are no surprises. Call us at (949) 629-5365 for a quick phone assessment.
We charge a diagnostic fee to cover the technician's time to assess the problem and provide an accurate estimate. If you approve the repair, the diagnostic fee is applied toward the total cost of the job. We'll always explain the fee structure upfront before your appointment.
We service all major appliance brands, including Whirlpool, GE, Samsung, LG, Maytag, Frigidaire, KitchenAid, Kenmore, Bosch, Thermador, Sub-Zero, Wolf, Viking, Miele, and Dacor. If you don't see your brand listed, call us — chances are we service it.
Yes. We service high-end and built-in appliances including Sub-Zero refrigerators, Wolf ranges, Viking dishwashers, and Thermador ovens. These units require specialized knowledge and experience — our technicians are trained to handle them with the care they deserve.
A good rule of thumb: if the repair cost is less than 50% of the appliance's current replacement value and the unit is under 10 years old, repair is usually the better choice. Our technicians will give you an honest assessment — we'll tell you if replacement makes more financial sense rather than push an unnecessary repair.
If a repair isn't feasible — due to unavailable parts, age of the unit, or the cost exceeding replacement value — we'll let you know clearly and honestly. You'll only pay the diagnostic fee; there's no charge for work that wasn't performed. We'd rather give you a straight answer than a costly short-term fix.
We do our best to accommodate urgent situations. Call us directly at (949) 629-5365 and explain the situation — a broken refrigerator full of food or a leaking washing machine can't always wait. We'll do our best to get a technician to you as soon as possible.
Clear access to the appliance — move any items stored on top of or directly in front of it. For refrigerators, you don't need to empty it unless we ask. Have the model number handy if possible (usually on a label inside the door or on the back panel). That's it — our technician handles the rest.
