## POST 2 — LG exhaust temperature sensor (educational / part spotlight)

**Photo:** `lg_dryer_exhaust_temperature_sensor.jpeg`

**Geotag:** Orange County, California (educational, not site-specific)
**Best posting window:** Fri 10 AM – 12 PM Pacific (educational content carries into weekend)

### Caption

> This tiny part is why an LG dryer stops heating 👀
>
> It's the **exhaust temperature sensor** (thermistor). It tells the control board how hot the air leaving the drum is. When it fails open, the board cuts heat to protect the dryer — so the drum keeps spinning, but clothes come out cold and damp.
>
> Signs your exhaust sensor is on its way out:
> 🔸 No heat or weak heat
> 🔸 Cycles ending early
> 🔸 tE / tEMP error on the display
> 🔸 Clothes still wet after a full cycle
>
> It's a ~30-minute swap for a tech — and far cheaper than replacing a heating element that wasn't actually the problem. Diagnose first, then replace what's actually failed.
>
> Got an LG dryer acting up? Link in bio or DM us.

## POST 3 — Maytag gas dryer thermostat replacement

**Photo:** `maytag_dryer_thermostat_changed.jpeg`

**Geotag:** Specific city if known, else Orange County, California
**Best posting window:** Mon 9–11 AM Pacific (Monday is peak laundry-breakdown call volume)

### Caption

> Maytag gas dryer — thermostat changed ✅
>
> This one came in as "won't get hot enough." The high-limit thermostat on the burner housing had drifted out of spec, so the dryer was cutting heat too early and taking 2–3 cycles to finish a load.
>
> A few things we check on every gas-dryer call:
> 🔧 Gas dryers run two thermostats — a cycling thermostat and a high-limit safety. When clothes need 2–3 cycles, one of them is usually the suspect.
> 🔧 Restricted vent lines cause thermostats to fail prematurely. We check vent flow every time.
> 🔧 New thermostat + a clean vent path = back to one-cycle dry times.
>
> If your dryer used to dry in 45 minutes and now takes 90+, that's a service call worth making.
>
