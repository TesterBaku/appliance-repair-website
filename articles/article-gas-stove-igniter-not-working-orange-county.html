Gas Stove Igniter Not Working? 5 Causes and Fixes

How Gas Stove Igniters Work

Gas stove igniters use a ceramic spark electrode positioned a few millimeters from each burner head. When you turn the knob to the ignite position, a central spark module sends a high-voltage pulse to all igniters simultaneously. The spark jumps from the ceramic tip to the metal burner head and ignites the gas flowing from the burner ports.

Two distinct symptoms point in opposite directions and tell you where to start your diagnosis:

  • Clicking but not lighting: the spark is reaching the igniter, but combustion is not starting at the burner. Focus first on the igniter tip or burner cap.
  • Completely silent (no click): the electrical side is not firing. Focus on the igniter switch for that burner or the central spark module.

Knowing which symptom applies before opening anything up saves time and avoids unnecessary disassembly.

The 5 Causes: Diagnosis by Symptom

Cause 1 (Most Common)

Food Debris on the Igniter Tip

Symptom: one or more burners click audibly but the flame never catches. Most common after a boil-over or a heavy cooking session.

The ceramic igniter tip sits a few millimeters from the burner head. Food residue, cooking grease, and starchy water coat that tip and break the clean electrical path the spark needs to jump reliably. Even a thin crust of carbonized grease can reduce spark intensity below the threshold needed to ignite gas consistently. This is the most common gas stove igniter complaint by a wide margin.

Fix: Remove the grate and burner cap. Clean the ceramic igniter tip with a dry toothbrush. Work around the base with a wooden toothpick for packed debris. Do not use water directly on the igniter. Let it air-dry for at least 20 minutes before testing.

Cause 2

Moisture in the Igniter Assembly

Symptom: all burners click rapidly when you activate one knob, intermittent sparks that die immediately, or unprompted clicking after you cleaned the cooktop.

Water from a boil-over reaching the igniter housing, or water from surface cleaning, creates a conductive path that bleeds the spark away before it can jump to the burner head. When moisture enters an igniter switch housing, the switch can activate continuously even with no knob turned.

Fix: Keep the range off with good ventilation for 30 minutes. For deeper moisture, set the oven to its lowest temperature setting for 10 minutes to gently warm the interior, then off. Do not use the burners during the drying period. If clicking continues after thorough drying, moisture has reached the switch housing and a technician is needed.

Cause 3

Misaligned or Off-Center Burner Cap

Symptom: a visible spark fires from the igniter, but the burner still does not light. This is most common after the cooktop was cleaned and the cap was removed and replaced.

The burner cap must sit flat and centered over the burner head ports. When it is rotated even slightly or tilted, it can physically block the igniter port so the spark fires but cannot reach the gas stream. The igniter is functioning correctly; the geometry between the spark and the gas is off.

Fix: Remove the grate, lift the cap, and reseat it flat and centered. It should drop into position without force. Many caps have an alignment notch that should face the igniter. Test before replacing the grate.

Cause 4

Failed Igniter Switch (One Silent Burner)

Symptom: exactly one burner produces no sound at all when its knob is turned, while all other burners spark and light normally.

Each burner position has its own igniter switch that signals the spark module to fire when the knob reaches the ignite detent. Switches wear out from repeated actuation or from moisture infiltration at the knob shaft. A failed switch leaves that single burner completely silent while the rest of the range operates normally. This is the most reliable way to distinguish a switch failure from a module failure.

Replacing a switch requires removing the knob and bezel panel to access the switch housing inside the cooktop. This is not user-serviceable on most modern ranges without appliance repair experience.

Repair cost: $100 to $250 parts and labor.

Cause 5

Failed Spark Module (All Burners Affected)

Symptom: all burners are completely silent at once, or all burners click continuously with no knob engaged.

The spark module is the central control board that generates the high-voltage pulse for every igniter simultaneously. When it fails in one direction, it stops firing entirely and all burners go silent. When it fails in the other direction, it locks into continuous firing mode and all burners click without any knob being turned. Both conditions affect every burner at once, which is the key indicator that distinguishes a module failure from a single-switch failure.

A module locked in continuous firing mode will damage the igniter ceramics over time. Turn off power to the range at the breaker if this is happening and schedule service promptly.

Repair cost: $150 to $350 parts and labor, depending on brand and model.

Gas smell is a separate emergency. If you detect a gas odor near the range, do not attempt to light a burner, run a diagnostic, or flip a light switch. Leave the home immediately, leave the door open behind you, and call your gas utility company from outside. This is a gas supply or gas valve issue, not an igniter issue, and requires the utility company to respond before any repair work.

What You Can Safely Check Yourself

Before calling a technician, work through this short list. Each step takes under five minutes and rules out the most common causes.

  • Clean the igniter tip. Use a dry toothbrush on the ceramic tip and a wooden toothpick for packed debris around the base. Do not use water or metal tools.
  • Reseat the burner cap. Remove the grate, lift the cap, and reseat it flat and centered. The cap should drop into position without force.
  • Let it dry. If you cleaned the cooktop with water in the last hour, wait 30 minutes before testing. Residual moisture is one of the most common reasons a just-cleaned stove will not light.
  • Check the circuit breaker. Igniters draw from a standard 110V circuit. A tripped breaker cuts power to the spark module entirely, silencing all igniters. Reset the breaker and test before assuming module failure.
  • Confirm the gas shutoff valve is open. The valve behind or beneath the range should be parallel to the gas line (fully open). If other gas appliances in the home also fail, the issue is with the supply line, not the stove.

When to Call a Technician

If none of the self-checks restore ignition, the cause is inside the electrical assembly. Signs a service call is the right next step:

  • All burners are completely silent after confirming the breaker is on
  • Clicking continues on its own with no knob turned
  • One specific burner has been silent for more than a day despite cleaning and cap realignment
  • The igniter sparks visibly but the gas supply is confirmed open and the burner cap is correctly seated
  • The stove is clicking on its own and you want to stop further igniter damage

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is my gas stove clicking but not lighting?

Clicking without ignition almost always means the ceramic igniter tip is dirty or wet. Food debris and cooking grease coat the tip and reduce spark intensity below the ignition threshold. Clean the igniter tip with a dry toothbrush, let it dry for 20 minutes, and retest. If clicking continues without ignition after cleaning, the igniter switch or spark module likely needs service.

Why does my gas stove igniter keep clicking on its own?

Unprompted clicking with no knob turned is almost always moisture in the igniter switch housing or a failed spark module. Water from cleaning or a boil-over is the most common cause. Set the oven to its lowest temperature for 10 minutes to dry it out, then retest. If clicking persists after drying, the switch or module has likely failed. Turn off power at the breaker to protect the igniter ceramics while you wait for service.

Is it safe to use my stove if one burner igniter is not working?

You can use functioning burners normally while one igniter is out of service. For the non-functioning burner, do not attempt to light it manually while the knob is on, as unburned gas can accumulate. If you do not smell gas when you turn the knob to ignite, the safety valve is closed and other burners are safe to use. If you smell gas near the range, do not use the stove and call your gas utility.

Can I replace a gas stove igniter myself?

Cleaning the igniter tip is a safe and worthwhile first step for any homeowner. Replacing the igniter switch or spark module involves disassembling the cooktop panel and disconnecting electrical connectors, and is not recommended without appliance repair experience. Incorrect installation can cause continuous arcing or prevent the safety interlock from functioning correctly.
