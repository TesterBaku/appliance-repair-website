Gas Range vs Electric Range Repair Costs in Orange County (2026)

Home › Blog › Range Repair Cost Guide

Gas Range Repair Costs

Gas ranges are mechanically simpler on the cooktop side than electric glass-top models. The most common repairs involve the ignition system, the gas supply components, and the oven cavity.

Ignition system repairs

When a burner clicks but won't light, or one burner goes silent while others work, the cause is usually an igniter switch or spark module. These are the most common gas range service calls in Orange County.

Which Type Costs More to Repair?

The honest answer depends on what broke.

Gas ranges cost less to repair when the problem is on the cooktop. Igniter switches, spark modules, and gas valves are purpose-built mechanical or electromechanical components with straightforward replacement procedures. The most common gas cooktop repair (igniter switch) runs $100 to $250, which is lower than the equivalent glass-top repair for the same symptom category.

Electric glass-top ranges can cost significantly more if the cooktop surface cracks. Replacing the glass-ceramic surface on a mid-range electric range runs $300 to $700 in parts alone, making it the single most expensive common repair on either fuel type. If the surface cracks due to impact, most manufacturers treat it as cosmetic and do not cover it under warranty.

Oven cavity repairs (bake element, temperature sensor, control board) are roughly equivalent in cost across both types. The oven components in a gas range and an electric range serve the same function and carry similar price points. A control board replacement on a Whirlpool gas range runs about the same as on a Whirlpool electric range of the same tier.

Premium brand note. For Wolf, Viking, Thermador, or other luxury ranges, repair costs run 25% to 75% higher than for standard residential brands. OEM parts for premium brands are brand-specific, often require authorized distributor sourcing, and carry longer lead times. This applies equally to gas and electric models in the premium tier. See our Wolf range repair cost breakdown for a premium-brand example. For Viking oven issues specifically, our Viking oven not heating guide covers causes and repair costs in detail.

When to Repair vs Replace

The standard rule in appliance repair is the 50% threshold: if the repair cost exceeds 50% of the cost of a comparable new range, replacement is typically the better long-term value.

For context, mid-range gas and electric ranges in Orange County typically replace at $800 to $2,500 new. That puts the 50% threshold at $400 to $1,250. Most single-component repairs, including igniter systems, gas valves, and heating elements, fall well below that threshold.

Scenarios where replacement deserves serious consideration:

  • The range has two or more simultaneous component failures
  • The range is 15 or more years old and a major component (control board, gas valve) has failed
  • A glass-ceramic cooktop surface cracked on a range already showing control board or element issues
  • The repair cost for a premium brand exceeds 50% of a new comparable model (less common, given premium replacement prices)

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it more expensive to repair a gas range or an electric range?

It depends on the repair. Gas ranges generally cost less for cooktop repairs (igniter, gas valve, burner grate) because the parts are less expensive than glass-top electric components. Electric ranges with glass-ceramic cooktops cost more if the surface cracks or a radiant element beneath it fails. For oven cavity repairs such as control board or temperature sensor, costs are similar across both types.

How much does gas range repair cost in Orange County?

Gas range repair in Orange County typically costs a $99 flat diagnostic fee (applied toward the repair), $100 to $350 for an igniter system repair, $300 to $600 for a gas valve, and $350 to $750 for a control board. Most gas range repairs run $200 to $450 total. Estimates vary by brand and diagnosis; a final quote is provided before any work begins.

How much does electric or glass-top range repair cost in Orange County?

Electric range repair in Orange County typically costs $150 to $400 for a surface element or burner switch, $300 to $700 to replace a cracked glass-ceramic cooktop surface, and $350 to $750 for a control board. Glass-top repairs tend to run higher than gas equivalents because the cooktop surface itself is a significant parts cost. Estimates vary by brand and diagnosis; a final quote is provided before any work begins.

When should I repair vs replace a range in Orange County?

The standard guideline is: if the repair cost exceeds 50% of the replacement cost of a comparable new range, replacement is typically the better value. Mid-range ranges replace at $800 to $2,500 new, putting the threshold at $400 to $1,250. Most single-component repairs fall below this threshold. If your range has multiple simultaneous failures or is 15 or more years old, replacement deserves consideration.

Do gas ranges or electric ranges last longer?

Both types average 13 to 15 years with normal use. Gas ranges have fewer electronic components on the cooktop side and no heating elements to burn out, but require gas valve and igniter maintenance over time. Electric glass-top ranges have fewer mechanical moving parts but are more vulnerable to surface damage and element failure. Premium brands in either fuel type often exceed 15 years with proper maintenance.
