What LG Washer Error Codes Actually Mean

LG washers are good at telling you what is wrong, if you know how to read the shorthand. Before you call, note the exact code on the display. It cuts the diagnosis time in half and points the technician straight at the problem part.

OE

Drain Error: Water Won't Leave the Drum

OE means the washer filled but can't pump the water out within its expected time. Nine times out of ten it is a clogged drain pump filter (that little door at the bottom-front of a front-loader), a kinked or blocked drain hose, or a failed drain pump. This is one of the quickest LG repairs, and it is the single most common code we clear in Aliso Viejo.

Very common
UE

Unbalanced Load: The Drum Won't Spin Up

UE (or uE) tells you the load shifted to one side and the machine won't spin at full speed because it would shake itself across the floor. Sometimes it is just a single heavy towel or a small load that bunched up. But if UE keeps returning on normal loads, the suspension springs, shock absorbers, or leveling feet are the real cause, and a washer that isn't level rides on the 1990s and 2000s slab floors common here.

Very common
LE

Motor or Rotor Sensor Fault

LE points to the motor or the rotor position sensor (the Hall sensor) on LG's direct-drive system. It often shows up after a chronically overloaded drum stresses the motor, or when the sensor connection loosens. Sometimes it clears with a reset, but a returning LE usually means the sensor or the stator wiring needs attention, not a part you want to guess at.

Moderate
IE / dE

Fill and Door-Lock Faults

IE means the washer isn't filling: check that both supply valves are open, then suspect the inlet valve or a clogged inlet screen. dE means the door isn't registering as locked, so the cycle never starts. On front-loaders that is usually the door lock assembly, a worn strike, or a door boot pushing against the latch. Neither is expensive, but both stop a wash cold.

Common
💡 Before you call: Write down the exact LG code, note whether it happens every cycle or only on big loads, and try one reset (unplug for 60 seconds). If the code comes right back, it is a hardware fault, not a glitch, and running the machine again on that error can turn a simple fix into a bigger one.

The LG Parts That Fail Most Often

Codes tell you where to look, but a few LG components account for the bulk of the repairs we do. Knowing them helps you understand the estimate before the technician even arrives.

  • Drain pump and filter. The number-one washer repair, LG or otherwise. Coins, hairpins, and lint jam the impeller or clog the filter, and the machine throws an OE. Clearing or replacing the pump is quick.
  • Door lock assembly. Front-load LG washers won't run if the door lock doesn't confirm it is latched. The little switch inside fails with use, triggering a dE code.
  • Door boot seal (front-loaders). The rubber gasket tears or grows mold, and a torn boot leaks water onto the floor. Aliso Viejo's hard water speeds up the mineral buildup that stiffens these seals.
  • Inlet water valve. When it fails or its screen clogs, the washer fills slowly or not at all and shows IE. A common, affordable swap.
  • Drum bearings. A loud grinding or roaring on the spin cycle points to worn bearings. This is a bigger job, and it is where the repair-or-replace conversation usually starts.
  • Main control board. Less common, but a board that scrambles cycles or throws random codes may need replacing, especially after a power surge.

Simple Habits That Keep an LG Washer Running

Most of the codes above are preventable. These habits matter even more here, where hard water leaves mineral deposits on hoses, valves, and seals:

  • Clean the drain pump filter every few months. On a front-load LG it is behind the small access panel at the bottom. Clearing it is the best defense against OE codes.
  • Don't overload the drum. Cramming loads is a leading cause of UE and LE faults, and it wears the bearings and suspension faster. Clothes should tumble freely.
  • Use HE detergent, and less of it. LG high-efficiency washers need low-suds detergent. Too much creates residue that fouls the pump and leaves odor in the drum.
  • Leave the door and dispenser open between washes. On front-loaders this lets the boot seal dry out and stops the mold and smell that hard water accelerates.
  • Keep the machine level. An unlevel LG washer walks and vibrates, throwing UE codes and wearing parts early. Adjust the feet until it doesn't rock.

What do LG washer error codes like OE, UE, and LE mean?

OE is a drain error: the washer can't push water out, usually because of a clogged drain pump filter, a kinked drain hose, or a failed pump. UE means the load is unbalanced and the drum can't spin safely, often from a small or bunched load or a machine that isn't level. LE is a motor or rotor position sensor fault on LG's direct-drive washers, sometimes triggered by an overloaded drum. A technician confirms the exact cause before replacing any part.
